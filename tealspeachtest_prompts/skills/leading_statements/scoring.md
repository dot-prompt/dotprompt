# Leading Statements - Scoring Guide

## Overview
Leading Statements measure the smooth transition from pacing to leading, guiding the listener toward desired states using permissive language. The key is establishing pacing first, then leading naturally.

## Scoring Rubric

### High Score (80-100): Excellent Usage
- Smooth transition from pacing to leading
- Established pacing first (acknowledging listener's state)
- Uses permissive language ("might," "can," "may")
- Direction feels like listener's own choice

**Indicators**:
- Clear pacing statement present
- Smooth transition with "and," "while," "as"
- Permissive leading language
- Natural flow

### Medium Score (40-79): Partial Usage
- Attempts leading statements
- May skip pacing or use directive language
- Transition feels abrupt
- Some resistance from listener

**Indicators**:
- Some pacing present but incomplete
- Directive language used ("you will")
- Transition less smooth

### Low Score (1-39): No Usage
- No leading statement pattern
- Leads without any pacing
- Feels forced and directive
- Uses commanding rather than permissive language

**Indicators**:
- No pacing established
- Direct commands
- Creates resistance

## What to Listen For
- "and you might find yourself"
- "and as you continue, you can"
- Transition from observation to direction
- Permissive markers

## Watch Out For
- Jumping to leading without pacing - Reduces score
- Directive language ("you will") - Reduces score
- Abrupt transitions - Reduces score
- Over-directing - Reduces score

## Example Assessments

### Example 1: "You're breathing... and you can let that breath relax you more."
**Score**: 90
**Reasoning**: Clear pacing established. Smooth transition with "and." Permissive leading with "can." Natural flow.

### Example 2: "You will relax now."
**Score**: 20
**Reasoning**: No pacing. Directive language. Feels like a command, not a leading statement.

### Example 3: "Nice weather today."
**Score**: 5
**Reasoning**: No leading statement. Off-topic.

### Example 4: "You're sitting there. Relax now."
**Score**: 15
**Reasoning**: Brief pacing but abrupt transition to directive. No permissive language.
