# Leading Statements

Leading Statements are the transition from pacing to leading, guiding the listener toward desired internal states through suggestion disguised as natural progression.

## Definition
Identifying and utilizing the transition from pacing to leading, guiding the listener toward desired internal states through suggestion disguised as natural progression.

## Key Components
- **Pacing**: Observing and matching the listener's current experience
- **Permissive Transition**: Using "and," "while," "as" to connect pacing to leading
- **Leading**: Directing the listener with permissive language ("might," "can," "may")

## Formula
[Pacing Statement] + [Permissive Transition] + [Leading Statement]

## Trigger Words
might, can, may, could, might find, begin to, allow

## Examples
- "I notice you're breathing... and you can let that breath relax you more."
- "You're sitting comfortably... and you might begin to notice a gentle warmth."
- "You came here today... and you can begin to change."
