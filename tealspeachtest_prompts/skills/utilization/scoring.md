# Utilization - Scoring Guide

## Overview
Utilization is scored based on the effective use of the listener's presented behavior, words, or state to build extreme rapport and seamlessly guide toward desired outcomes. The key is acknowledging everything without judgment.

## Scoring Rubric

### High Score (80-100): Excellent Usage
- Masterfully acknowledges and utilizes whatever the listener presents
- Creates extreme rapport by working with the listener's reality
- Transition feels natural: [Observe behavior] + [Normalize] + [Lead to suggestion]
- Nothing is ignored or rejected

**Indicators**:
- Clear observation of what listener presents
- Normalization without judgment
- Natural flow from observation to suggestion
- Real connection between behavior and outcome

### Medium Score (40-79): Partial Usage
- Attempts utilization but may ignore some aspects
- Transition may be slightly too directive
- Connection between behavior and suggestion not fully natural
- Some rapport building present

**Indicators**:
- Some acknowledgment present
- Partial connection to suggestion
- Slightly forced transition

### Low Score (1-39): No Usage
- No utilization pattern used
- Rejects or pathologizes listener's presentation
- Judges behavior negatively
- Fails to build rapport or create seamless transition

**Indicators**:
- Ignores what listener presents
- Too directive
- Negative judgment
- No transition

## What to Listen For
- Acknowledging listener's actual behavior/words/state
- Normalizing without judgment
- Natural flow from observation to suggestion
- Nothing is ignored or rejected

## Watch Out For
- **Ignoring presentation** - Breaks rapport
- **Being too directive** - Not natural
- **Creating unnatural connection** - Forced
- **Negative judgment** - Breaks acceptance

## Example Assessments

### Example 1: "I see you're fidgeting... that's perfectly okay... and as you fidget, you can notice how your body is telling you to relax."
**Score**: 90
**Reasoning**: Classic utilization. Clear observation (fidgeting), natural normalization ("perfectly okay"), smooth transition to suggestion. Creates extreme rapport.

### Example 2: "You're fidgeting, and that's okay, and you can relax now."
**Score**: 70
**Reasoning**: Some utilization present. Acknowledges fidgeting and normalizes, but transition is more directive. Connection less natural.

### Example 3: "Stop fidgeting and pay attention."
**Score**: 20
**Reasoning**: No utilization. Rejects the presentation entirely. Negative judgment. No rapport building.
