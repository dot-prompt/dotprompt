# Section 1: Skill Overview

## Core Content
Conversational Postulates are questions that function as commands or requests while preserving the appearance of choice. The listener processes the question and feels compelled to respond in alignment with the implied request.

## Key Points
- Questions that function as commands
- Preserve appearance of choice
- Use human tendency to answer questions
- Reduce resistance while guiding behavior

## Example
"Would you like to make yourself comfortable?" - Implies sitting down without commanding it.

## Comprehension Question
What makes conversational postulates different from regular questions?

## Follow-up Guidance
If they struggle, explain: Regular questions seek information, while conversational postulates imply a desired action. The question form softens what would otherwise be a command.

# Section 2: Psychological Mechanism

## Core Content
When someone asks a question, the human brain automatically begins processing how to answer it. This creates a cognitive "set" toward the implied action. The question form softens the command, reducing resistance while still guiding behavior.

## Key Points
- Automatic Processing: Brain starts working on the answer
- Reduced Resistance: Questions feel like invitations
- Maintained Autonomy: Listener feels they chose to act

## Example
"Could you notice your breathing?" - Listener begins noticing breathing to answer the question.

## Comprehension Question
Why does the question form reduce resistance compared to direct commands?

## Follow-up Guidance
If they struggle, explain: Questions feel like invitations, not orders. The listener maintains a sense of choice and autonomy.

# Section 4: Practical Applications

## Core Content
Conversational postulates can be used in therapy to guide processes, in sales to nudge decisions, and in leadership to facilitate action.

## Key Points
- Therapy: "Would you like to go deeper into that memory?"
- Sales: "Would you like to see how this could work?"
- Hypnotic: "Could you notice how your eyes are closing?"

## Comprehension Question
How would you use a conversational postulate with someone resistant to direct suggestions?

## Follow-up Guidance
If they struggle, suggest: Use the question form to maintain their sense of choice while gently guiding them.
