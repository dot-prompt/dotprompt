# Lost Performatives

Lost Performatives are statements of value or belief where the source of the judgment is deleted, making opinions sound like objective truths.

## Definition
Identifying and utilizing statements of value or belief where the source of the judgment is deleted, making opinions sound like objective truths.

## Key Components
- **Value Judgment**: Using words like "good," "important," "natural," "valuable"
- **Source Deletion**: Removing attribution ("I think," "Experts say")
- **Universal Truth Appearance**: Making opinions sound like objective facts

## Trigger Words
- It's good to...
- It's important to...
- It's natural to...
- It's valuable to...
- It's wise to...
- It's helpful to...

## Examples
- "It's good to relax before sleep."
- "It's important to breathe deeply."
- "It's natural to feel curious."
