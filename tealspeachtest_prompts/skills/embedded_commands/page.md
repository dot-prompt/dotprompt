# Embedded Commands

## Definition
An embedded command is a suggestion hidden inside a larger sentence so the listener's conscious mind hears a normal statement, while the unconscious mind picks up the instruction.

**Example:** "You might find that as you listen, you can begin to relax now."

The direct command is "relax now", but it's disguised inside a conversational sentence.

NLP teaches that the unconscious mind responds strongly to these subtle instructions—especially when they are marked by:
- tone shifts
- pauses
- slower speech
- gestures
- eye contact

The goal isn't manipulation—it's bypassing resistance and helping suggestions land more naturally.

## Core Effect on Listener
The conscious mind processes the surface meaning of the sentence while the unconscious mind detects and responds to the hidden command. This bypasses the critical filter that would reject direct suggestions.

- **Role in Milton Model**: Embeds commands within conversational language to bypass resistance
- **Opposite Meta Model Pattern**: Making the hidden command explicit ("Relax now!")

## When to Introduce
Embedded commands are a beginner-friendly technique. Used in therapy, coaching, sales, and leadership to deliver suggestions that feel like natural conversation.

## How It Should Sound
Natural, conversational—混入 the command seamlessly into the sentence. The listener shouldn't notice anything unusual.

## Common Mistakes
1. **Too Obvious**: Command stands out too much, conscious mind detects it
2. **No Marking**: Failing to use tone/pause/eye contact to emphasize the command
3. **Overembedding**: Every sentence has commands, sounds manipulative
4. **Direct Commands**: Just adding "please" doesn't disguise a command

## Two Micro-Examples
1. "You might find that as you listen, you can begin to relax now."
2. "Some people notice they naturally begin to trust their own judgment."

## Template Variations
### Simple Embed
"You can [command]"

### With Frame
"You might find that you can [command]"

### Complex
"[Frame] [permission phrase] [command]"

## Scoring Guidance
- **High Score (80-100)**: Commands hidden inside conversational sentences. Unconscious picks up the instruction while conscious hears normal speech. Natural flow maintained.

- **Medium Score (40-79)**: Some embedding present but commands may be slightly obvious, or flow is disrupted, or marking is inconsistent.

- **Low Score (1-39)**: Direct commands only ("Relax!"), no embedding, triggers resistance.

- **What to Listen For**: Hidden commands in longer sentences, "you can," "you might," commands disguised as observations.

- **Watch Out For**: Too obvious, breaking flow, direct commands, overembedding.
