# Embedded Commands - Scoring Guide

## Overview
Embedded Commands are scored based on the effective hiding of suggestions inside larger sentences. The conscious mind hears a normal statement while the unconscious picks up the hidden instruction.

## Scoring Rubric

### High Score (80-100): Excellent Usage
- Suggestions hidden inside conversational sentences
- Commands are clear but disguised
- Natural flow maintained
- Bypasses conscious resistance

**Indicators**:
- Commands embedded in larger statements
- Consciously sounds normal
- Unconscious receives instruction

### Medium Score (40-79): Partial Usage
- Some embedding present
- Commands may be too obvious
- Flow slightly disrupted
- Some resistance remains

**Indicators**:
- Partial hiding
- Commands partially visible
- Some awkwardness

### Low Score (1-39): No Usage
- Direct commands only
- No embedding
- Triggers resistance

**Indicators**:
- "Relax." "Trust me."
- No disguise
- Direct suggestions

## What to Listen For
- Hidden inside larger sentences
- "you can..."
- "you might..."
- "you may..."
- Commands disguised as observations

## Watch Out For
- **Too obvious** - Conscious catches it
- **Breaking flow** - Sounds unnatural
- **Direct commands** - "Relax." triggers resistance
- **Overembedding** - Every sentence has commands

## Example Assessments

### Example Response 1: "You might find that as you listen, you can begin to relax now."
**Score**: 95
**Reasoning**: Classic embedded command. "relax now" is hidden inside a conversational sentence. Conscious hears a question; unconscious receives command. Natural flow maintained.

### Example Response 2: "Relax now, please."
**Score**: 20
**Reasoning**: Direct command. No embedding. Triggers conscious resistance. "Please" doesn't disguise it.

### Example Response 3: "Some people relax."
**Score**: 5
**Reasoning**: No embedded command. Just a statement about other people. No suggestion directed at the listener.
