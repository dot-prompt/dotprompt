# Unspecified Verbs - Teaching Content

## Section 1: The Power of Vague Action

Unspecified verbs work by leaving the "how" open for the listener's unconscious to fill in. When you say "you'll change," the listener's mind immediately runs through possibilities based on their own experience. This makes the suggestion feel personalized rather than prescribed.

The key is vagueness about process while clarity about outcome. You want to specify what, but not how.

**Key Points:**
- Listener fills in their own method
- Increases buy-in ("I created this myself")
- Maintains therapeutic direction
- Makes suggestions feel personal

**Example:** "You'll change"
- Listener thinks: "How will I change? Through therapy? Meditation? Just by thinking?"
- All interpretations are valid
- The listener's unconscious picks their own path

---

## Section 2: Choosing the Right Verbs

Effective unspecified verbs are action words that don't require specific methods:

- **Good**: learn, change, improve, get better, grow, develop, discover, understand
- **Avoid**: explain (requires explanation), teach (requires teacher), show (requires demonstration)

The trick is finding verbs that imply an outcome without dictating the process.

**Key Points:**
- Choose verbs that imply outcomes
- Avoid verbs requiring specific methods
- Maintain therapeutic direction
- Test: "Can the listener fill in their own how?"

**Examples:**
- "You can learn" ✓ (learner defines method)
- "You can understand" ✓ (understanding is personal)
- "You can explain" ✗ (requires explanation method)

---

## Section 3: Common Mistakes

The main mistakes with unspecified verbs:

1. **Adding Too Many Specs**: "You'll change by practicing meditation daily"
2. **Using Specific Verbs**: Verbs requiring objects or methods
3. **Too Vague**: "Things will happen" (loses all direction)
4. **Wrong Direction**: Implying negative change

**Key Points:**
- Keep it vague about how, clear about what
- Choose universally applicable verbs
- Maintain positive direction
- Test for personal projection

**Pattern Stacking:**
- Unspecified Verb + Presupposition: "You'll change, which means improvement is coming."
- Unspecified Verb + Embedded Command: "You can learn, and you can allow that to happen now."
- Unspecified Verb + Cause-Effect: "You improve, and because you improve, confidence grows."

**Common Mistakes:**
1. Adding explanations of how
2. Using verbs requiring objects
3. Being so vague nothing is communicated
4. Negative implications
