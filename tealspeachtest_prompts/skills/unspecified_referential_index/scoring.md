# Unspecified Referential Index - Scoring Guide

## Overview
Unspecified Referential Index is scored based on the effective use of vague subjects that allow the listener to personally identify with the statement. The key is creating identification without specifying who.

## Scoring Rubric

### High Score (80-100): Excellent Usage
- Vague subjects that invite personal identification
- Markers: "People," "Some," "They," "One," "Many"
- Listener applies "people can" to "I can"
- Sounds universal and inclusive
- Maintains natural flow

**Indicators**:
- Appropriate vague subject chosen
- Personal identification happens automatically
- Statement feels general, not evasive

### Medium Score (40-79): Partial Usage
- Some vague subjects present
- May be too specific or slightly distancing
- Identification may be incomplete
- Some awkwardness

**Indicators**:
- Vague subjects used but not perfectly
- Some distance created
- Partial identification

### Low Score (1-39): No Usage
- No unspecified referential index pattern
- Specific subjects used (you, I, specific names)
- Creates distance or exclusion
- No identification happens

**Indicators**:
- Direct reference (you, I, him, her)
- "Those people" creates distance
- No universal application

## What to Listen For
- "People," "Some," "They," "One," "Many"
- General statements that apply to everyone
- Listener naturally thinks "I can too"

## Watch Out For
- **Too specific** - Breaks identification
- **"Those people"** - Creates distance
- **Wrong references** - Listener can't identify
- **Too vague** - Loses all meaning

## Example Assessments

### Example 1: "People can learn quickly when they find the right approach."
**Score**: 90
**Reasoning**: Classic unspecified referential index. "People" is vague and universal. Listener naturally thinks "I can too." Sounds natural and inclusive.

### Example 2: "Some people can learn quickly."
**Score**: 70
**Reasoning**: Some unspecified referential index present. "Some people" works but slightly less universal than "people." Still effective.

### Example 3: "You can learn quickly."
**Score**: 5
**Reasoning**: Direct reference. No unspecified referential index. No deletion of subject. No opportunity for listener to identify themselves—they're just being told.
