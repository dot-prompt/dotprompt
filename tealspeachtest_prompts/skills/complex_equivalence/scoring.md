# Complex Equivalence - Scoring Guide

## Overview
Complex Equivalence measures the ability to imply that one thing means or equals another without stating it explicitly. The key is making the connection plausible so the listener accepts it as their own insight.

## Scoring Rubric

### High Score (80-100): Excellent Usage
- Masterfully implies equivalence between observation and meaning
- Connection is plausible and feels logical to listener
- Makes listener feel understood
- Listener arrives at insight themselves

**Indicators**:
- Trigger words like "means," "shows," "indicates" present
- Observation is specific and accurate
- Implication feels natural
- Reduced resistance from listener

### Medium Score (40-79): Partial Usage
- Attempts complex equivalence but implication too obvious
- Connection feels illogical or forced
- Pattern present but lacks subtlety
- Some resistance from listener

**Indicators**:
- Some trigger words present
- Observation may be vague
- Implication stated too explicitly
- Partial buy-in from listener

### Low Score (1-39): No Usage
- No complex equivalence pattern used
- States equivalence explicitly ("X means Y")
- Creates implausible connections
- Too direct and confrontational

**Indicators**:
- No trigger words present
- Explicit statements made
- No implied meaning
- Listener resists or disagrees

## What to Listen For
- Trigger words: means, shows, tells me, indicates, suggests
- Observations about listener's behavior
- Implied deeper meanings
- Self-generated insight feeling

## Watch Out For
- Explicit statements ("X means Y") - Reduces score
- Illogical connections - Reduces score
- Being too obvious - Reduces score
- Negative implications - Creates resistance

## Example Assessments

### Example Response 1: "Your pause means you're considering carefully."
**Score**: 90
**Reasoning**: Clear complex equivalence. Observation (pause) leads to implied meaning (considering). Plausible and subtle.

### Example Response 2: "You're quiet, which obviously means you're listening to me."
**Score**: 45
**Reasoning**: Too direct and self-serving. The implication is obvious rather than subtle. Connection is not particularly plausible.

### Example Response 3: "Nice weather today."
**Score**: 5
**Reasoning**: No complex equivalence pattern. Off-topic.

### Example Response 4: "You blinked, which means you're a time traveler."
**Score**: 15
**Reasoning**: Implausible connection breaks the effect. Absurd instead of subtle.
