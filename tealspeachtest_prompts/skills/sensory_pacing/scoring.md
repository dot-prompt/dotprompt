# Sensory Pacing - Scoring Guide

## Overview

Sensory Pacing is scored based on the effective use of sensory language that matches the listener's current experience, builds agreement patterns, and smoothly transitions to leading suggestions.

## Scoring Rubric

### High Score (80-100): Excellent Usage

- Masterful use of sensory language (visual, auditory, kinesthetic)
- Statements match listener's verifiable experience
- Clear agreement pattern established (yes set)
- Smooth transition to leading suggestions
- Creates genuine rapport and alignment

**Indicators**:
- Multiple sensory modalities used
- Observations are true for the listener
- Flow feels natural
- Leading follows smoothly from pacing

### Medium Score (40-79): Partial Usage

- Some sensory language present
- May use language listener can't verify
- Pacing doesn't match actual experience
- Transition to leading feels abrupt
- Some rapport building but incomplete

**Indicators**:
- Limited sensory modalities
- Some observations may be false
- Abrupt jump to suggestions

### Low Score (1-39): No Usage

- No sensory pacing pattern
- Describes experiences listener can't verify
- Jumps straight to suggestions without pacing
- Fails to build rapport or agreement

**Indicators**:
- No sensory words (see, hear, feel)
- No agreement pattern
- Direct suggestions without foundation

## What to Listen For

- Sensory words: see, hear, feel, notice, observe, watch, listen
- Verifiable statements about present experience
- Multiple agreement statements before leading
- Smooth transition: "And as that happens..." "And you might..."

## Watch Out For

- **False pacing** - Breaks trust
- **One modality** - Less effective
- **No leading** - Builds rapport but no change
- **Sound scripted** - Breaks naturalness

## Example Assessments

### Example Response 1: "As you sit there reading these words, you can see the screen in front of you, and you may notice the sounds around you. And as you continue reading, you might begin to relax."

**Score**: 95

**Reasoning**: Excellent sensory pacing with multiple modalities (kinesthetic, visual, auditory). Observations are verifiable. Builds yes set. Smooth transition to leading suggestion about relaxation.

### Example Response 2: "You can see clearly now. And you should relax."

**Score**: 50

**Reasoning**: Some sensory language ("see clearly") but not true pacing. No build-up of agreement. Abrupt transition to command. Incomplete pattern.

### Example Response 3: "You need to relax."

**Score**: 5

**Reasoning**: No sensory pacing. No observation of current experience. Direct command without foundation. No rapport building.
