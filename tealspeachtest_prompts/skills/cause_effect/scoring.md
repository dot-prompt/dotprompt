# Cause Effect - Scoring Guide

## Overview
Cause Effect is scored based on the effective use of the cause-effect pattern where stating a cause implies a particular effect. The listener's brain automatically accepts the connection between cause and effect without questioning whether it's valid.

## Scoring Rubric

### High Score (80-100): Excellent Usage
- Clear cause-effect relationship stated
- Creates sense of inevitability
- Bypasses resistance
- Effect feels like natural consequence

**Indicators**:
- Cause stated, effect implied
- Sounds like logical consequence
- No resistance triggered
- "Because X, Y" format

### Medium Score (40-79): Partial Usage
- Some cause-effect present
- Relationship may feel forced
- Some resistance
- Not fully natural

**Indicators**:
- Partial cause-effect
- Connection weak
- Some awkwardness

### Low Score (1-39): No Usage
- No cause-effect pattern
- Direct statements only
- No implied connection

**Indicators**:
- No causal language
- Direct suggestions
- No inevitability

## What to Listen For
- "Because..."
- "As you..., ...happens"
- "When you..., ..."
- "By..., you..."

## Watch Out For
- **Weak connection** - Cause doesn't logically lead to effect
- **Forcing** - Sounds artificial
- **Direct only** - No implication, just commands
- **Questionable causation** - Listener may question validity

## Example Assessments

### Example Response 1: "Because you're listening, you can learn."
**Score**: 90
**Reasoning**: Clear cause-effect. Listening causes learning. Creates inevitability. Brain accepts connection without resistance. Sounds natural.

### Example Response 2: "Listen and learn."
**Score**: 30
**Reasoning**: Direct command pairing, not cause-effect. "Listen" doesn't cause "learn"—they're just并列. No implication of natural consequence.

### Example Response 3: "Learn new things."
**Score**: 5
**Reasoning**: No cause-effect at all. Direct suggestion. No causal connection established. Triggers resistance.
