# Mind Reading - Scoring Guide

## Overview
Mind Reading is scored based on the effective use of statements about the listener's internal state that create a powerful sense of being understood. The key is demonstrating apparent understanding of what the listener is thinking or feeling.

## Scoring Rubric

### High Score (80-100): Excellent Usage
- Statements about listener's internal state
- Creates sense of being understood
- Builds deep rapport
- Sounds confident but not arrogant
- Appropriate to context

**Indicators**:
- Claims to know thoughts/feelings
- Creates connection
- Feels empathetic

### Medium Score (40-79): Partial Usage
- Some mind reading present
- May sound uncertain or hesitant
- May be slightly off-target
- Some rapport built

**Indicators**:
- Partial claims to know internal state
- Mixed results
- Some awkwardness

### Low Score (1-39): No Usage
- No mind reading pattern
- No claims about listener's internal state
- No rapport building through apparent insight

**Indicators**:
- No statements about thoughts/feelings
- Generic statements
- No connection created

## What to Listen For
- "I know you're..."
- "You must be feeling..."
- "I can see you're..."
- Claims about internal experience

## Watch Out For
- **Being wrong** - Accuracy matters
- **Sounding arrogant** - Not "I know better than you"
- **Overusing** - Every sentence becomes mind reading
- **Being too specific** - Guess broadly, not details

## Example Assessments

### Example Response 1: "I know you're feeling excited about this, and that excitement is going to help you learn faster."
**Score**: 90
**Reasoning**: Clear mind reading claiming to know the listener's feeling. Builds rapport. Adds value by connecting excitement to learning. Sounds confident.

### Example Response 2: "You might be feeling something about this."
**Score**: 40
**Reasoning**: Weak mind reading attempt. Very vague ("something"). Sounds uncertain rather than confident. Doesn't create strong connection.

### Example Response 3: "This is a good training program."
**Score**: 5
**Reasoning**: No mind reading. Generic statement about the program. No claim about listener's internal state. No rapport building.
