# Presuppositions - Scoring Guide

## Overview
Presuppositions are scored based on the effective use of statements that assume something to be true without explicitly stating it. The listener accepts the presupposed content because it's embedded in a larger, acceptable statement.

## Scoring Rubric

### High Score (80-100): Excellent Usage
- Clear presuppositions embedded
- Assumed content is acceptable to listener
- Creates inevitability
- Natural flow maintained

**Indicators**:
- "As you..." "Before you..." "When you..."
- Presupposed content feels true
- Listener accepts the assumption

### Medium Score (40-79): Partial Usage
- Some presuppositions present
- May be slightly forced
- Some assumptions may feel questionable
- Some awkwardness

**Indicators**:
- Partial presupposing
- Connection less natural
- Some resistance

### Low Score (1-39): No Usage
- No presuppositions
- Direct statements only
- No assumptions embedded

**Indicators**:
- Explicit statements only
- No "as," "when," "before"
- All content stated directly

## What to Listen For
- "As you..."
- "When you..."
- "Before you..."
- "The moment..."
- "Your ability to..."

## Watch Out For
- **Questionable assumptions** - Listener may reject
- **Too many** - Sounds manipulative
- **Direct only** - No presupposing
- **Breaking flow** - Sounds unnatural

## Example Assessments

### Example Response 1: "As you continue reading, you'll discover your own wisdom."
**Score**: 90
**Reasoning**: Classic presupposition. Assumes: (1) you will continue reading, (2) you have wisdom, (3) you will discover it. All presuppositions are positive and acceptable. Creates inevitability.

### Example Response 2: "You'll discover wisdom."
**Score**: 30
**Reasoning**: Direct statement. No presupposition. "You'll discover" is stated directly rather than assumed. No inevitability created.

### Example Response 3: "Wisdom exists."
**Score**: 5
**Reasoning**: Generic statement about wisdom. No presupposition about the listener. No personal application. No assumption about listener's experience.
