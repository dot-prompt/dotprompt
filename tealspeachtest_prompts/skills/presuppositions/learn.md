# Presuppositions - Teaching Content

## Section 1: What Are Presuppositions?

Presuppositions are assumptions embedded in statements that the listener must accept to make sense of the sentence. The presupposed content enters the mind without conscious evaluation or resistance.

**Key Points:**
- Assumptions embedded in sentences
- Listener must accept to understand the sentence
- Enters mind without resistance
- Central to Ericksonian hypnosis

**Example:** "When you learn this technique, you'll improve." - Presupposes: you will learn, you will improve

---

## Section 2: Why They Work

The brain processes the presupposition to understand the sentence. The listener can't reject the presupposition without rejecting the sentence itself—which feels confusing and inefficient.

**Key Points:**
- Brain accepts presupposition to extract meaning
- Rejecting it would mean not understanding
- Cumulative effect: multiple presuppositions build reality

**Example:** "As you continue, the results get better." - Must accept "you will continue" to make sense of "results get better"

---

## Section 3: Trigger Words

Common presupposition triggers: When you..., After you..., How quickly will you..., Which... will you..., While you..., As you..., Since you're...

**Key Points:**
- "When you..." presupposes it will happen
- "After you..." presupposes completion
- "How quickly will you..." presupposes it will happen
- "Which... will you" presupposes choice will be made

**Example:** "How quickly will you achieve your goal?" - Presupposes: you will achieve a goal

---

## Section 4: Construction Method

To construct presuppositions: identify your goal (what assumption to install), select trigger word, embed the assumption, refine for naturalness.

**Key Points:**
1. Goal: What assumption do you want to install?
2. Trigger: Use "when," "after," "as"
3. Embed: Make goal part of trigger condition
4. Refine: Make surface meaning make sense

**Example:**
- Goal: assume listener will relax
- Trigger: "when"
- Construction: "When you relax, you'll notice..."

**Common Mistakes:**
- Too obvious presuppositions that feel manipulative
- Negative presuppositions that work against you
- Stacking too many presuppositions causing confusion
- Surface meaning doesn't make sense
