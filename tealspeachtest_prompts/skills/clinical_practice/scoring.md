# Clinical Practice - Scoring Guide

## Overview
Clinical Practice measures the integration of multiple NLP skills in a therapeutic context. The key is demonstrating fluid use of rapport building, pacing, reframing, and language patterns while staying present with the patient and adapting to their needs.

## Scoring Rubric

### High Score (80-100): Masterful Integration
- Demonstrates masterful integration of multiple NLP skills
- Stays present with the patient throughout the session
- Notices and responds to emotional shifts
- Adapts communication style fluidly to patient needs
- Achieves clinical outcomes through appropriate skill deployment

**Indicators**:
- Smooth transitions between techniques
- Patient shows visible signs of rapport
- Multiple NLP patterns used naturally
- Clinical outcomes achieved

### Medium Score (40-79): Partial Integration
- Shows competence with some NLP skills
- May rely too heavily on one technique
- Struggles to adapt to patient's emotional state
- Progress is made but not optimal
- Some elements present but not fully developed

**Indicators**:
- Uses some techniques effectively
- May miss emotional cues
- Some flexibility in approach
- Partial outcomes achieved

### Low Score (1-39): No Integration
- Fails to establish rapport
- Loses track of patient's emotional state
- Uses inappropriate or robotic language
- Demonstrates no NLP skill integration
- Completely off-topic or ineffective

**Indicators**:
- No rapport established
- Single-technique reliance or none
- Ignores emotional signals
- No discernible progress

## What to Listen For
- Rapport indicators (matching, pacing)
- Acknowledgment of patient reality
- Flexible language patterns
- Emotional attunement
- Ability to navigate resistance
- Smooth transitions between techniques

## Watch Out For
- Rigidity in approach - Reduces score
- Losing the patient - Reduces score
- Single-technique reliance - Reduces score
- Ignoring emotional signals - Reduces score
- Forced or unnatural language - Reduces score
- Over-using techniques - Can sound manipulative

## Example Assessments

### Example Response 1: "I notice you're sitting here today, and as you do, you might become aware of how ready you are for this change."
**Score**: 90
**Reasoning**: Clear integration of pacing ("I notice you're sitting here"), leading ("as you do"), and reframing ("ready for change"). Natural delivery, establishes rapport, and creates opportunity for clinical outcome.

### Example Response 2: "You're going to feel better because I said so."
**Score**: 25
**Reasoning**: Direct command instead of integrated approach. No rapport building, no pacing, robotic language. Fails to adapt to patient's reality.

### Example Response 3: "Nice weather today."
**Score**: 5
**Reasoning**: No attempt to use clinical NLP skills. Completely off-topic, no therapeutic relevance.

### Example Response 4: "I see you're nervous about your presentation. Let's just skip that topic and talk about something else."
**Score**: 30
**Reasoning**: Acknowledges the issue but fails to work with it. No pacing, no reframing, no use of language patterns to help client. Avoids rather than addresses the resistance.
