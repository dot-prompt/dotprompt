# Clinical Practice

## Definition
Applying various NLP techniques fluently in a therapeutic or clinical context to achieve breakthrough results with a patient.

## Core Effect on Listener
This is the general integration mode where you can practice any and all NLP skills you have learned. In this mode, the focus is on the overall clinical outcome rather than mastering a single specific pattern.

## When to Introduce
This skill is appropriate when the practitioner has mastered foundational NLP patterns and is ready to integrate multiple techniques in a therapeutic setting.

## How It Should Sound
The practitioner should demonstrate fluid integration of:
- **Rapport Building**: Matching and leading
- **Pacing**: Acknowledging the client's current reality
- **Reframing**: Shifting perspectives
- **Language Patterns**: Using Milton Model or Meta Model patterns as appropriate

## Common Mistakes
1. Relying on a single technique
2. Losing track of the patient's emotional state
3. Failing to adapt to the patient's archetype
4. Using robotic or scripted language patterns

## Two Micro-Examples
1. "I notice you're sitting here today, and as you do, you might become aware of how ready you are for this change."
2. "You came in with a concern, and while we're exploring it, let's also notice what strengths you already have."

## Template Variations
### Therapeutic Integration
Using multiple NLP patterns to navigate client resistance and unlock progress markers.

## Scoring Guidance
- **High Score (80-100)**: User demonstrates masterful integration of multiple NLP skills including rapport building, pacing, reframing, and language patterns. They stay present with the patient, notice and respond to emotional shifts, and adapt communication style fluidly. Clinical outcomes are achieved through appropriate skill deployment.

- **Medium Score (40-79)**: User shows competence with some NLP skills but may rely too heavily on one technique or struggle to adapt to the patient's emotional state. Progress is made but not optimal.

- **Low Score (1-39)**: User fails to establish rapport, loses track of patient's emotional state, uses inappropriate or robotic language patterns, or demonstrates no NLP skill integration.

- **What to Listen For**: Rapport indicators (matching, pacing), acknowledgment of patient reality, flexible language patterns, emotional attunement, ability to navigate resistance.

- **Watch Out For**: Rigidity in approach, losing the patient, single-technique reliance, ignoring emotional signals, forced or unnatural language.
