# Tag Questions - Scoring Guide

## Overview
Tag Questions are scored based on the effective use of statements followed by question tags that invite agreement. The key elements are: statement + appropriate tag + falling inflection (expectation of yes).

## Scoring Rubric

### High Score (80-100): Excellent Usage
- Statement followed by tag inviting agreement
- Tags like "aren't you," "isn't it," "doesn't it"
- Falling inflection (expects yes)
- Sounds conversational, not mechanical
- Creates internal affirmation

**Indicators**:
- Tag matches statement appropriately
- Tone conveys expectation of yes
- Flow feels natural

### Medium Score (40-79): Partial Usage
- Some tag questions present
- May use negative tags incorrectly
- Tone may be wrong (rising inflection)
- Sounds slightly mechanical

**Indicators**:
- Tags present but not fully effective
- Mixed results
- Some awkwardness

### Low Score (1-39): No Usage
- No tag question pattern
- Sounds like genuine questions
- Tags don't invite agreement
- No agreement priming

**Indicators**:
- No tags used
- Rising inflection (real questions)
- No internal affirmation

## What to Listen For
- "aren't you," "isn't it," "doesn't it," "won't you"
- Falling inflection on tags
- Statement + tag as one utterance
- Internal affirmation

## Watch Out For
- **Overusing** - Sounds interrogative
- **Negative tags** - Invites disagreement ("don't you want to fail?")
- **Rising inflection** - Turns into real question
- **Breaking flow** - Two separate utterances

## Example Assessments

### Example Response 1: "You're feeling good, aren't you?"
**Score**: 90
**Reasoning**: Classic tag question. Statement "You're feeling good" followed by "aren't you?" Falling inflection expects yes. Creates internal affirmation. Sounds conversational.

### Example Response 2: "You're feeling good? aren't you?"
**Score**: 40
**Reasoning**: Statement and tag separated, breaking flow. Not one smooth utterance. Loses effectiveness.

### Example Response 3: "Are you feeling good?"
**Score**: 5
**Reasoning**: Genuine question with rising inflection. No tag question pattern. Requires actual answer, doesn't create internal affirmation.
